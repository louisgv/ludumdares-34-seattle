DubHacks 2015: Important Information

DubHacks is just around the corner! Please read this email carefully for important tips and information leading up to the event this weekend.

Travel

If you're coming into the US from another country, remember to bring a valid passport/visa!

Getting here from the airport:

  • You can take a combination of the LINK Light Rail and public buses to the University of Washington campus for under $10

  • If you’re traveling in a group, you can split a cab from the airport. Please note that ride-sharing services such as Lyft and Uber are not available from the Sea-Tac International Airport.

Where should I park? Parking on the UW campus is complimentary from noon on Saturday until 6:00am Monday. There is a parking garage under Red Square (Central Plaza) that you may use. Other parking spots + prices: tiny.cc/dubhacks-parking

Reimbursement is only available if it was offered to you in your decision email. Receipts for airfare and ground transport must be emailed to info@dubhacks.co as soon as they are received. Please allow several weeks for processing reimbursement which will be sent to the Venmo or PayPal account specified on your confirmation form.

If you’re taking the bus from Vancouver you should have received an email with more detailed bussing times and locations in your inbox.

Check in

Check in opens at the main doors of Kane Hall at the University of Washington campus at 3:00pm Saturday. You must bring a valid student or government-issued identification card to be admitted.

Hacking

The schedule can be found at our website at http://15f.dubhacks.co/, and more detailed information about sponsor-hosted workshops will become available as we move closer to the event.  We begin with opening ceremony at Kane Hall at 5:00pm and hacking will commence soon after in Mary Gates and Johnson halls. You must submit your hack by 5:00pm on Sunday and we will wrap up with the project expo, judging, and closing ceremony from 8:00-9:00pm.

If this is your first hackathon and you’re feeling overwhelmed, don’t worry! There will be opportunities to find teammates at the event and we recommend you read our guide for first-time hackers.

If you haven’t already, join our DubHacks 2015 Facebook group to find team members, coordinate travel, and stay updated about hackathon events. We have two more pre-event workshops for DubHacks attendees this week: Wearables on Wednesday and “Making Command Monkey” on Thursday.

What to bring

Just the essentials: a laptop, chargers, valid ID, toothbrush, sleeping bag, etc. If you don't have a laptop we have a few that you can borrow for the duration of the event. Please do not bring: Weapons, illegal substances, alcohol, or pets.

Hardware can be checked out from our dedicated hardware lab. This equipment is available at a first come, first serve basis and we cannot guarantee the availability of any equipment to any specific group.

We hope you’re as excited about DubHacks as we are! This will be the largest event yet with 600 competing students. Please let us know if you have any questions/comments/concerns about the event at info@dubhacks.co. Happy hacking!
